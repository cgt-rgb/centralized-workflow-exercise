# Pasta carbonara recipe


## Ingredients

- spaghetti (regular or whole grain)
- guanciale
- salt
- pepper
- parmigiano reggiano
- eggs


## Instructions

1. Bring a large pot of well-salted water to a boil.
2. Cut the guanciale into small strips or cubes.
3. In a bowl, crack the eggs and whisk them together with a generous amount of finely grated parmigiano reggiano and plenty of black pepper. Mix until smooth and creamy.
4. Place the guanciale in a cold pan, then turn the heat to medium. Cook slowly until the fat renders and the guanciale is crisp at the edges. Turn off the heat and keep the rendered fat in the pan.
5. Cook the spaghetti (regular or whole grain) in the boiling water until al dente, following package time as a guide.
6. Just before draining the pasta, reserve about 1 cup of pasta water.
7. Transfer the hot spaghetti directly into the pan with guanciale and toss to coat in the rendered fat.
8. Let the pasta cool for about 20 to 30 seconds so the eggs do not scramble.
9. Add the egg and cheese mixture to the pasta and toss continuously until the sauce becomes glossy and clings to the spaghetti.
10. If needed, add a small splash of reserved pasta water, a little at a time, to loosen and emulsify the sauce.
11. Taste and adjust with more pepper and grated parmigiano reggiano.
12. Serve immediately.
